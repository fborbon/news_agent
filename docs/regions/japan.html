Japan's LDP Approves Retrial Reform Bill, Cabinet Decision Due May 15

Japan's ruling Liberal Democratic Party approved the Justice Ministry's final proposal to revise the criminal retrial system, paving the way for a cabinet decision on May 15 and submission to the current Diet session. The revised bill — prompted by the 2024 retrial acquittal of death-row inmate Hakamada Iwao — tightens the requirements for prosecutors to appeal retrial-opening decisions and embeds the near-prohibition of such appeals in the main body of the Code of Criminal Procedure rather than supplementary provisions. Some LDP members expressed reluctance but accepted the compromise as a step forward, vowing to continue pushing for stronger reforms in parliament.
