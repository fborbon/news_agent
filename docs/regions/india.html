Cabinet approves ₹37,500 crore coal gasification scheme to boost energy security

The Union Cabinet approved the Scheme for Promotion of New Surface Coal/Lignite Gasification Projects with an outlay of ₹37,500 crore, targeting the incentivisation of 25 projects aimed at gasifying approximately 75 million tonnes of coal and lignite. The scheme seeks to reduce India's import dependence on LNG, urea, ammonia and methanol by leveraging the country's vast domestic coal reserves, estimated at 389 billion tonnes. It supplements an existing ₹8,500 crore viability gap funding scheme approved in 2024 and is framed against the backdrop of global energy supply concerns stemming from the West Asia crisis. The government views coal gasification as a cleaner, more energy-secure alternative to continued fossil fuel imports.

Amul hikes milk prices by ₹2 per litre across India, effective May 14

Gujarat Cooperative Milk Marketing Federation (GCMMF), which markets under the Amul brand, announced a ₹2 per litre price hike on fresh milk across India effective May 14, 2026 — the first such increase since May 2025. The revision, representing a rise of approximately 2.5% to 3.5%, was attributed to higher input costs including cattle feed, packaging film and fuel. GCMMF also announced that procurement prices paid to farmers have been raised by ₹30 per kg fat, a 3.7% increase, noting that nearly 80 paise of every consumer rupee is passed on to milk producers. The cooperative stated the hike remains below the average food inflation rate.
