Scientists Find Ancient Stardust Trapped in Antarctic Ice

Researchers have discovered traces of iron-60, a radioactive isotope produced only in supernova explosions, in Antarctic ice core samples dating back 40,000 to 81,000 years, as part of the European EPICA project. The findings, published in Physical Review Letters, suggest that Earth has been passing through a Local Interstellar Cloud — a region of gas, dust and plasma formed by past supernova activity — for at least 80,000 years. Scientists say the concentration of iron-60 in older ice layers is significantly higher, pointing to a continuously evolving interstellar environment around our Solar System.
