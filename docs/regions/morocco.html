Morocco Temporarily Suspends Soft Wheat Imports Amid Record Harvest

Morocco will suspend soft wheat imports between June 1 and July 31, 2026, according to Abdelkader Alaoui, president of the National Milling Federation, as reported by Reuters. The decision follows seven consecutive years of drought that forced Morocco to rely heavily on foreign markets, and reflects a dramatic recovery in domestic production. Official estimates put the 2026 cereal harvest at approximately nine million tonnes, nearly double the previous season's figure. The move marks a significant turning point in Morocco's food supply strategy.
