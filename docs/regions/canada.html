A proposed $66-million class-action settlement has been reached with the British Columbia government over its decades-long use of 'birth alerts,' a practice that allowed hospitals and child-welfare agencies to flag pregnant patients — disproportionately Indigenous women — without their knowledge or consent, often resulting in newborns being apprehended at birth. The province formally ended the practice in September 2019, acknowledging the harm it caused. B.C. Ontario AI medical scribes 'hallucinated' errors in doctor notes, auditor general warns

Ontario Auditor General Shelley Spence has found that AI transcription tools evaluated for use by provincial doctors produced inaccurate, incomplete, or fabricated medical information — including false drug prescriptions and unsolicited treatment referrals — during government testing. Bill C-22 could allow police to remotely activate microphones on personal devices

A 'lawful access' bill currently before the House of Commons, Bill C-22, would require Canadian electronic service providers to give law enforcement streamlined access to subscriber data, location information, and potentially the ability to remotely activate microphones on phones, laptops, and smart speakers. Canada's retroactive asylum law leaves 2SLGBTQ+ claimants facing deportation

Bill C-12, passed in late March, bars refugee claimants from filing if more than one year has elapsed since their first entry into Canada, with the law applied retroactively to claims linked to entries dating back to June 24, 2020. The reform is leaving thousands of 2SLGBTQ+ individuals — including those who only came to terms with their sexual orientation after arriving in Canada — potentially ineligible for protection and facing deportation to countries where they face violence or criminalization. Advocates argue the law fails to account for the complex, often delayed circumstances under which queer claimants recognise their need for asylum. The government has said the changes are designed to reduce the backlog in the refugee system.
