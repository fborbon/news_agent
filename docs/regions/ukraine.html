Ukraine Drone Strike Shuts Down Russia's Perm Oil Refinery in the Urals

Russia's Perm oil refinery — located approximately 1,460 km east of Moscow in the Ural Mountains — has fully halted processing following a Ukrainian drone strike on May 7, industry sources told Reuters. Three primary crude distillation units were taken offline immediately after the attack, with additional secondary units also shut down; one unit had already been idle since a prior strike on April 30. The facility, owned by Lukoil, processed around 12.6 million tons of crude oil in 2024 (roughly 250,000 barrels per day). Repairs are expected to take weeks, and Ukraine's Unmanned Systems Forces confirmed the attack using long-range Antonov An-196 'Liutyi' drones.
